Who We Are

Welcome to HERD

HERD — Helping Environment and Resilient Development — is a newly established Non-Profit Company (NPC) dedicated to addressing environmental challenges while fostering community resilience. HERD focuses on community development, environmental protection, and the promotion of sustainable practices.

In the heart of Wonderkop, a village nestled in Marikana — where brave mine workers lost their lives fighting for fair wages — HERD is weaving together environmental stewardship and community empowerment. Despite sitting atop one of the richest mineral belts in the world (South Africa produces 90% of the globe's platinum), the community continues to grapple with poverty, high unemployment, and inadequate infrastructure.

Recognising the deep scars left by the Marikana massacre, HERD stepped in to heal, uplift and ignite a sense of resilience and possibility for a brighter future — fostering dialogue and collaboration with various stakeholders.

Our Vision

A World in Harmony with Nature

We envision a world where communities live in harmony with the environment, take proactive measures to restore ecosystems, and are equipped to thrive despite environmental challenges. Through environmental education, leadership development, and sustainable innovation, HERD aims to create resilient and empowered communities that lead the charge in protecting our planet for future generations.

Our Mission

Empowering Communities for a Sustainable Future

HERD is dedicated to creating a sustainable future by empowering communities with the knowledge, tools, and skills necessary to protect the environment and build resilience in the face of climate challenges. We believe in the power of education, community action, and innovation to foster environmental stewardship and promote sustainable livelihoods. Our approach integrates environmental protection with socioeconomic development.

How We Work

The HERD Cycle

Our self-sustaining strategy transforms communities from dependence to independence through four interdependent pillars.

1
Develop — Implement educational and healthcare projects that answer direct community needs.
2
Produce — Give beneficiaries access to the economy through skills training and enterprise development.
3
Trade — Within 12 months, ESD beneficiaries begin trading, earning a solid, self-sustaining income.
4
Reinvest — Beneficiaries use their income to pay for healthcare, training, and education — reinvesting in their community.

The Challenge

Environmental Realities of Marikana

Despite immense mineral wealth beneath the surface, Wonderkop faces serious environmental hazards that HERD is working to address.

💧

Water Shortage

Community members face acute water scarcity, often forced to source water from unsafe mine-adjacent sources.

🏭

Air Pollution

Smelters and mining operations release pollutants that degrade air quality and cause respiratory health issues.

🌬️

Dust Management

Mine-related dust affects visibility, livestock, crops, and the health of Wonderkop's residents.

♻️

Environmental Pollution

Improper waste disposal and lack of sanitation infrastructure contribute to widespread environmental degradation.

🌡️

Climate Change

Shifting rainfall patterns and increasing temperatures threaten food security and agricultural livelihoods.

⚠️

Tailings Dam Safety

Mining tailings dams pose ongoing safety risks to the community, requiring active monitoring and management.
